This project is an interactive hand-tracking experience where you use your hand to avoid falling rain. The goal is simple: keep your hand safe by moving it around the screen as the rain gets heavier.

Concept

I wanted to make a small playable piece that turns a natural element (rain) into a digital challenge. It mixes a calm atmosphere with a slightly stressful task, so the viewer can feel the tension between nature and control.

Interaction

  • Move your hand in front of the camera to control the hand position on the canvas.
  • Try to avoid the falling rain drops.
  • Use the slider to change the rain intensity (more drops = harder).
  • Click the button to turn on sound for a more immersive experience.

How it works

The system uses a webcam and hand tracking (ml5.js) to detect hand movement. The tracked hand position is sent into a p5.js sketch, which draws the rain and checks collisions. Sound is handled with p5.sound and starts only after the user presses the sound button.
